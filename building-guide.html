XRP Robot Assembly Guide

Follow this step-by-step guide to assemble your XRP robot. The entire process takes about 15 minutes.

Assembly Video

Watch the complete assembly video, or follow the written instructions below with timestamps to jump to specific sections.

What's in the Kit

Before you begin, make sure you have all the parts from your XRP kit:

Robot Chassis

Single-piece design with a rail system on top for easy component attachment.

Robot Controller

The brain of your robot with an RP2350 microprocessor. Reads sensors, runs programs, and drives motors.

Motors with Encoders

Two motors that drive the robot, with built-in sensors to measure wheel rotation.

Battery Case

Holds 4 AA batteries. Rechargeable batteries are recommended.

Ultrasonic Rangefinder

Measures distance to objects in front of the robot using sound waves.

Reflectance Sensor

Detects light and dark surfaces for line following.

Servo Motor

Moves to preset angles for the robotic arm.

Servo Arm

Attaches to the servo for grabbing or pushing objects.

Wheels & Tires

Two wheels with o-ring tires for traction.

Casters

Two ball casters for the front of the robot.

Assembly Steps

1

Insert the Robot Controller

Video: 1:18

Position the controller board with the battery connector facing toward the back of the robot. Remove any tape from the board connectors first.

Snap the corners of the board into the pockets on the chassis. Make sure it's seated firmly.

2

Install the Battery Pack

Video: 1:39

Thread the battery cable through the cutout in the chassis.

Push the battery pack against the chassis fingers until it clicks into place.

3

Add the Battery Cover

Video: 2:29

Align the tabs on the battery cover with the slots on the chassis.

Snap the cover into place.

4

Insert the Casters

Video: 3:06

Press the white ball casters into the holes at the front of the chassis.

They should snap in securely.

5

Mount the Wheels

Video: 3:22

Press the wheels onto the motor shafts.

Align the flat side of the wheel hub with the flat side of the motor shaft.

6

Add the Tires

Video: 3:45

Stretch the o-ring tires into the grooves on each wheel.

These provide traction for your robot.

7

Connect Motor Cables

Video: 3:52

Insert the cable connectors into each motor.

Make sure they click in securely.

8

Install the Motors

Video: 4:09

Snap the motors into the chassis from the bottom.

The motors should click firmly into place.

9

Connect Motors to Controller

Video: 4:43

Connect the left motor cable to the "Motor L" connector on the controller.

Connect the right motor cable to the "Motor R" connector.

10

Wire the Sensors

Video: 5:11

Important: Sensor cable wiring is the most common cause of problems. Be careful here!

Attach the cables to both the reflectance sensor and the rangefinder. When removing connectors, always pull on the plastic shell, not the wires.

11

Mount Sensor Brackets

Video: 5:44

Attach the sensor brackets to the front of the chassis.

The reflectance sensor faces down toward the ground. The rangefinder faces forward.

12

Connect Sensors to Controller

Video: 6:55

Connect the reflectance sensor cable to the "Line" connector on the controller.

Connect the rangefinder cable to the "Range" connector.

13

Attach Servo Bracket

Video: 7:29

Insert the ball end of the servo bracket into the upper slot on the chassis.

Snap the bottom onto the rail system.

14

Mount the Servo

Video: 7:54

Snap the servo motor into the bracket.

It should fit snugly.

15

Connect Servo Cable

Video: 8:06

Connect the servo cable to the "Servo 1" connector on the controller.

16

Install Servo Arm

Video: 8:27

Press the servo horn into the slot on the arm.

Make sure the orientation is correct.

17

Mount Arm to Servo

Video: 8:45

Press the arm onto the servo shaft.

The arm should have about 180 degrees of motion range.

Testing Your Robot

Once assembly is complete, run the built-in verification test to confirm all sensors and motors are working properly.

Watch the verification test (12:44 in the video)

Ready to Code!

Your robot is now assembled. Head to the Code Editor to start programming your XRP!
